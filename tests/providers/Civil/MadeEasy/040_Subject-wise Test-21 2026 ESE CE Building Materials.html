Not attempted

Superplasticizers or super water-reducers

Correct answer: D
Solution

(d)
Superplasticizes increase the workability of green or fresh concrete to have higher flowability
without increasing the water content which results in faster rate of concrete placement, ease of
placement in relatively poorly accessible locations without vibration, good shutter finish for highly
reinforced concrete members, and reduction in cement content. Super-plasticisers, high-range waterreducing
admixtures, are the class of plasticizers which have fewer deleterious effects. Advantages
of water reduction in hardened state of concrete are increased strength, density, durability, volume
stability, abrasion resistance, reduced permeability and cracking.

Consider the following statements:
Sand in cement mortar is used for
1. increasing the strength.
2. reducing the shrinkage.
3. decreasing the surface area for the binding material.
4. decreasing the quantity of cement.
Which of the above statements are CORRECT?

Correct answer: D
Solution

(d)
Sand is an inert filler in mortar. In small quantities (up to an optimum), the inclusion of wellgraded
sand can help the composite mix carry compressive load, but in general and does not
primarily increase strength but its main role is to provide body, reduce shrinkage and reduce
cement requirement. If sand content is increased beyond the optimum then the available cement
paste to coat the particles and bond them gets reduced, and strength decreases. So it is incorrect to
state, as a general rule, that sand is used to increase strength.

Consider the following statements regarding the strength of brick masonry:
1. The type of mortar used influences the overall strength of the masonry.
2. Masonry built with lime mortar generally gains strength at a slower rate and achieves a lower final strength compared to masonry built with cement mortar.
3. The strength of the mortar is a primary factor in determining the compressive strength of the masonry assembly.
Which of the above statements is/are correct?

Correct answer: A
Solution

(a)
• The bond between bricks and mortar is crucial. Different mortars (e.g., cement, lime, or a
combination) have different bonding properties, strengths, and flexibilities, all of which directly
affect the masonry's strength and behavior under load.
• Lime mortar has a slower chemical setting process (carbonation) and typically results in a
lower ultimate compressive strength than the faster-hydrating, stronger cement mortar.
However, lime mortar offers better flexibility and vapor permeability.
• While the strength of the bricks themselves is important, the mortar is often the weaker component
in the system. The mortar's strength and its ability to distribute stress between bricks are key
factors that dictate the overall compressive strength of the masonry wall or column.

Which defect in timber is characterized by a swirl or distortion of the wood grain and is caused by an injury or stress to the tree during its early growth?

Correct answer: B
Solution

(b)
• Burls: These are irregular, rounded swellings of knots found on the trunk or branches of a
tree. They are caused by some form of stress, such as an injury, virus, or fungus, at a young
age. The grain pattern is wildly distorted and interlocked.
• Knots: These are not injuries but are simply the bases of branches that were embedded in the
tree as it grew.
• Upset: Also known as compression failure, this is a defect caused by crushing of the wood
fibers during its growth (e.g., by wind) or during felling.
• Shakes: These are cracks that separate the wood fibers, often occurring due to uneven seasoning
(drying) or internal forces as the tree ages.

Consider the following statements regarding the high-pressure steam curing of concrete:
1. It increases the compressive strength of concrete.
2. It reduces the drying shrinkage of the cured concrete.
3. It increases the speed of hydration reaction.
Which of the above statements is/are correct?

Correct answer: A
Solution

(a)
Steam curing under pressure (also known as autoclaving) is an accelerated curing process.
• The combination of heat and pressure significantly accelerates the hydration process and
promotes the formation of stronger calcium silicate hydrate compounds, leading to higher
ultimate compressive strength compared to normal curing.
• The high-pressure steam process results in a more stable and denser cement paste structure.
This reduced porosity makes the concrete less susceptible to losing moisture and therefore
minimizes drying shrinkage.
• Heat is a catalyst for chemical reactions. The elevated temperature in a steam chamber
dramatically increases the kinetic energy of water and cement particles thereby speeding up
the rate of hydration.

Which of the following aggregates gives maximum strength in concrete?

Correct answer: D
Solution

(d)
Rounded particles have smooth surfaces and low surface area per unit volume, so less cement
paste is needed to coat them and to achieve workability. They also pack more easily (less interlock),

strength in concrete.

(b) Both Statement I and Statement II are correct but Statement II is not the correct explanation of Statement I.
(c) Statement I is true but Statement II is false.
(d) Statement I is false but Statement II is true.

Statement I: Rice husk ash, when processed under controlled combustion, exhibits high pozzolanic activity and can improve the durability of concrete.
Statement II: Controlled combustion retains silica in a noncrystalline form with high surface area, which enhances its reactivity similar to silica fume.

Correct answer: A
Solution

(a)
Rice husk ash contains a high proportion of silica. If burned in open fields, most silica becomes
crystalline (less reactive). But when processed under controlled combustion, the silica remains
amorphous (noncrystalline) with fine particles and high surface area (50-60 m²/g), making it highly
pozzolanic. This behavior is very similar to silica fume, thereby improving strength and durability
of concrete.
