Q15 Not checked
Technical • ELECTRONICS ENGINEERING • +2 / -0.66
Not attempted

In a multiple choice question paper having 30 questions, the marking scheme is as under:
+5 marks for each correct question;
+1 mark for each unanswered question;
No score for wrong response.
A group of four students named Arun, Bishal, Chetan and Dhawal gave the following statements:
Arun: My score is 141
Bishal: I have scored 144
Chetan: My score is 147
Dhawal: I scored 139.
Only one of the students has got a valid score. What is the name of that student?

Correct answer: A
Solution

(a)
• If Chetan has scored 147, it means he has done 29 questions correctly i.e. 29 × 5 = 145 but the
30th question can give him 0 or 1 leading to possible score of 145 or 146. Thus, Chetan’s score
is not valid.
• Bishal has scored 144 implies that he could have done 28 questions correctly leading to a score
of 28 × 5 = 140 but remaining 2 questions can possibly give score of 2 or 1 or 0 depending on
his incorrect attempts. This leads to possible scores of 140 or 141 or 142 leading to possibility
of option ‘a’ being correct option. Thus, Arun's score of 141 is vaid [28 questions answered
correctly, 1 unaswered question and 1 wrongly answered].
• Dhawal having scored 139 means that he has attempted 27 questions correctly leading to a
score of 27 × 5 = 135 but remaining 3 questions can possibly give maximum score of 3 i.e. total
possible score = 138. Thus, Dhawal’s score is not valid.
