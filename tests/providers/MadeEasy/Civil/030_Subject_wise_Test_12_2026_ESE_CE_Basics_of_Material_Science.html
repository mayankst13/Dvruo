Consider the following statements:
1. Slip occurs more readily when the atoms are far away from each other.
2. The direction in which the dislocation moves is called the slip direction, and the planes impacted by the slip are called the slip planes.
3. The stress required to induce slip increases exponentially with increasing interplanar spacing.
Which of the above statements is/are correct?

Correct answer: A
Solution

(a)
Slip occurs more readily when the atoms are close together. The stress required to induce slip
increases exponentially with increasing interplanar spacing. Slip requires the breaking of bonds,
so materials with strong covalent bonds (such as polymers) are resistant. Materials with ionic
bonds (such as metal oxides) are resistant because of larger interplanar distances and the repulsions
caused when particles with like charge are forced to pass close to each other.
The direction in which the dislocation moves is called the slip direction, and the planes impacted
by the slip are called the slip planes. Together the slip planes and slip direction make up the slip
system. Sufficient energy to break the bonds and move the atoms is required for slip to occur, and
the dislocation will move in the direction that requires the least energy.

Consider following statements regarding vacancy defects :
1. Arise due to quick cooling and thermal vibration of atoms at high temperature.
2. This defect generally has more chance of occurrence in atoms having low atomic packing factor.
3. It leads to increase in randomness of the structure.
4. It helps in movement of atoms through lattice.
Which of the above statements are correct?

Correct answer: B
Solution

(b)
• A vacancy is a point defect where an atom is missing from its normal site in the crystal structure.
At sufficiently high temperatures, the atoms of the solid vibrate vigorously about their regular
positions. If an atom acquires enough energy, then it leaves its site completely resulting in a
vacancy.
• Interstitial defect occurs if the atomic packing factor of crystal structure is low.
• The presence of a missing atom disrupts the perfect, periodic arrangement of the atoms, thereby
increasing the entropy of the crystal.
• The presence of atomic allows the migration of atoms adjacent to the vacancies, thus resulting
in movements of atoms through lattice.
Hence, statements 1, 3 and 4 are correct.

Consider the following statements regarding atomic packing in crystals:
1. Engineering materials are made up entirely of small crystals or grains.
2. The grains are stuck together meeting at grain boundaries.
3. The crystal system can be conceptualized as packing atoms two-dimensionally in atomic planes and stacking these planes on top of one another to give crystals.
Which of the above statements is/are correct?

Correct answer: D
Solution

(d)
Many engineering materials (almost all ceramics and metals) are made up entirely of small crystals
or grains in which atoms are packed in regular, repeating three dimensional patterns. The grains
are stuck together meeting at grain boundaries. The three dimensional packing pattern can be
conceptualized as packing atoms two-dimensionally in atomic planes and stacking these planes on
top of one another to give crystals.

Which of the above statements is/are correct?

Correct answer: C
Solution

(c)
Type-I superconductors have only one critical magnetic field (HC). When the applied field exceeds
this value, Super conductivity is destroyed abruptly.
Type-II super conductors : They have two critical fields (HC1 and HC2).
Between HC1 and HC2, they are in a mixed state, still carrying supercurrent. This makes them suitable for high field electromagnets.
