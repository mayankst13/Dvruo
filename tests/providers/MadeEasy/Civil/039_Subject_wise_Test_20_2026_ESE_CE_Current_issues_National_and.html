Consider the following statements regarding the Census in India:
1. The first synchronous decennial census in India was conducted in 1881 under the supervision of W.C. Plowden.
2. Independent India’s first census took place in 1951, and since then, it has been conducted in the first year of every decade.
3. The Census of India Act of 1948 specifies exact timing and periodicity of Census.
Which of the statements given above are correct?

Correct answer: A
Solution

(a)
• The first synchronous (simultaneous across regions) decennial census in India was indeed conducted in 1881.
• It was carried out under the supervision of W.C. Plowden, who was then the Census Commissioner of India.
• The first census after independence was held in 1951.
• Since then, India has conducted a decennial census in every first year of the decade.
• The 2021 census was delayed due to COVID-19 but this does not make the statement incorrect.
• The Census Act of 1948 does not mandate any fixed timing or periodicity. It empowers the government to decide when to conduct a census, but does not require it to be every 10 years.

Statement (I): Statement (I): Variable Rate Reverse Repo (VRRR) is a monetary policy instrument employed by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) to maintain short-term interest rate stability.
Statement (II): It is primarily used to absorb surplus liquidity from the banking system.

Correct answer: B
Solution

(b)
• The Variable Rate Reverse Repo (VRRR) is a monetary policy tool. By conducting VRRR auctions, the RBI influences short-term interest rates and ensures they remain close to the policy rate, helping maintain rate stability.
• The VRRR is a liquidity absorption tool. When there is excess liquidity in the banking system (e.g., due to large inflows or government spending), the RBI uses the VRRR to drain out this surplus, thereby avoiding inflationary pressures and maintaining monetary stability.
